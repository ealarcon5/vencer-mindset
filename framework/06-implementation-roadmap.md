# Phase 6: Implementation Roadmap

## What This Phase Does

Strategy without execution is just entertainment. This roadmap turns everything in Phases 1–5 into a concrete weekly action plan for the first 90 days. It is intentionally sequenced — the invisible layer work must come before the visible layer, and the foundation must come before the offers.

**The rule:** Do not rush to offers. Rushed offers built on a vague identity require hustle to fill. Patient offers built on deep identity fill through resonance.

---

## The 90-Day Arc

```
DAYS 1–30   FOUNDATION
            Build the invisible layer. No publishing required.
            Purpose: get clear on identity, architecture, first landmark.

DAYS 31–60  EMERGENCE
            Start publishing. Introduce your world.
            Purpose: establish the visible layer, build the depth platform.

DAYS 61–90  ACTIVATION
            Introduce your first offer.
            Purpose: open the offer, run the async application process,
            close your first cohort or first high-touch clients.
```

---

## Month 1: Foundation (Days 1–30)

The goal of Month 1 is complete clarity on your invisible layer. Nothing ships publicly that is consequential. You are building the foundation that everything else will stand on.

### Week 1: Identity Work

| Day | Task | Time |
|-----|------|------|
| 1 | Complete Phase 1 (Identity Foundation) — full worksheet | 60 min |
| 2 | Review Phase 1 answers. Cut anything that sounds like what someone else would say. | 30 min |
| 3 | Write your personal narrative: the path you've walked that others haven't. Raw, honest, 500–1,000 words for yourself only. | 60 min |
| 4 | Identify your 3 most deeply held principles (from Phase 1C). Write a short explanation of each one from lived experience. | 45 min |
| 5 | Complete Phase 2 (Brand Architecture) — brand lens and brand focus | 45 min |
| 6 | Test your brand lens: list 10 interests you have and show how each fits inside it. If 3+ don't fit, revise the lens. | 30 min |
| 7 | Rest. Let the identity work settle. |  |

**Week 1 output:** 
- Completed Phase 1 worksheet
- Completed Phase 2 worksheet
- Your personal narrative document
- Your principle set

---

### Week 2: World Architecture

| Day | Task | Time |
|-----|------|------|
| 8 | Complete Phase 3 (World Building) — content pillars and distribution stack | 60 min |
| 9 | Choose your primary platform (discovery) and your depth platform. Set up your newsletter / Substack if not already live. | 60–90 min |
| 10 | Map the content-to-trust ladder for your specific situation | 30 min |
| 11 | Begin Phase 4 (Landmark System) — brainstorm 10 landmark concepts | 45 min |
| 12 | Score landmark candidates and identify top 2–3 | 30 min |
| 13 | Fully develop your first landmark (language, visual, system) | 60 min |
| 14 | Rest / review. |  |

**Week 2 output:**
- Completed Phase 3 worksheet
- Depth platform live (newsletter / Substack)
- First landmark fully developed with language, visual anchor, and system

---

### Week 3: Offer Design

| Day | Task | Time |
|-----|------|------|
| 15 | Complete Phase 5 (Offer Architecture) — primary offer design | 60 min |
| 16 | Design your entry-level offer | 45 min |
| 17 | Write your 7-question async application | 30 min |
| 18 | Write your offer page — what it is, who it's for, outcome, price, how to apply. No design needed. A Google Doc is fine. | 60 min |
| 19 | Develop your second landmark concept | 45 min |
| 20 | Write a private document: "The reason I'm building this." Not a mission statement — a real explanation of why this matters to you, what you're done with, and what you're moving toward. | 45 min |
| 21 | Rest. |  |

**Week 3 output:**
- Completed Phase 5 worksheet
- Offer page draft (private)
- Async application ready
- Second landmark in development
- Your "why" document

### Week 4: Content Foundation

| Day | Task | Time |
|-----|------|------|
| 22 | Write your "world introduction" piece — a long-form essay or video script that introduces your worldview, your brand lens, and what kind of world you're building. This is not a launch. | 90 min |
| 23 | Write your first newsletter / Substack post — introduce yourself to your depth layer. Not an announcement. A genuine dispatch about where you are and what you're thinking. | 60 min |
| 24 | Outline your first Landmark content piece (the full video / essay / whatever format for your discovery platform) | 45 min |
| 25 | Review everything. Make sure your identity document, brand architecture, landmarks, and offers are coherent with each other. | 60 min |
| 26 | Refine your profile / bio on your primary platforms using your brand lens language | 30 min |
| 27 | Buffer / catch-up day | — |
| 28 | Month 1 review: What's clear? What's still vague? Write it down. | 30 min |

**Week 4 output:**
- World introduction piece drafted
- First newsletter written and ready to send
- First Landmark content outlined
- Profile / bio updated
- Month 1 review notes

**Month 1 milestone check:**
- [ ] Identity document complete and feels true
- [ ] Brand lens passes the 10-interest test
- [ ] Brand focus is specific — I can picture a real person
- [ ] Depth platform live
- [ ] At least 2 landmarks developed
- [ ] Primary offer designed with price, format, and application ready
- [ ] Entry offer concept defined

## Month 2: Emergence (Days 31–60)

Month 2 is about bringing your invisible layer into the visible world. You start publishing consistently and introduce your landmarks. No offers are promoted yet — this is pure world-building.

### Week 5–6: Publish the Foundation

| Priority | Task |
|----------|------|
| HIGH | Publish your world introduction piece on your discovery platform |
| HIGH | Send your first newsletter (don't wait for a big list — start with whoever's there) |
| HIGH | Produce and publish your first Landmark content piece |
| MEDIUM | Publish 2–3 shorter pieces that support your first landmark (across pillars) |
| MEDIUM | Set up a simple content calendar: 1 major piece + 1–2 supporting pieces per week |
| LOW | Begin building your second landmark content piece |

**Consistency target:** 1 major piece (landmark or vision content) per week on your discovery platform, 1 newsletter per week on your depth platform.

---

### Week 7–8: Deepen the World

| Priority | Task |
|----------|------|
| HIGH | Publish your second Landmark content piece |
| HIGH | Write 3–4 newsletter issues from your Pillar 2 (Path) — stories, failures, lessons |
| HIGH | Continue weekly content on your discovery platform |
| MEDIUM | Start a simple tracking document: which content is getting real resonance? (Not just views — comments, DMs, replies that say "this is exactly what I needed") |
| MEDIUM | Have 5–10 real conversations with people in your audience — email replies, DM responses. Understand their language. |
| LOW | Draft your offer introduction content piece |

**Month 2 milestone check:**
- [ ] At least 2 landmarks published
- [ ] Depth platform active with 4+ issues sent
- [ ] I know what content is resonating (from real human responses, not metrics)
- [ ] I have real language from my audience to inform how I talk about my offer
- [ ] Offer introduction content is drafted

---

## Month 3: Activation (Days 61–90)

Month 3 is when you open the first offer. You have 60 days of world-building behind you. The people in your depth layer have been seeing your landmarks, reading your newsletter, and developing a relationship with your worldview. Now you invite.

### Week 9–10: The Invitation

| Priority | Task |
|----------|------|
| HIGH | Publish the landmark that directly sets up the offer (the content that names the problem your offer solves) |
| HIGH | Send 2–3 newsletter issues that deepen the identity your offer delivers |
| HIGH | Open the application — post your offer page link once, with a genuine invitation, not a launch |
| MEDIUM | Respond to every application within 48 hours (Loom video or written) |
| MEDIUM | Continue weekly content (do not go dark during the offer period) |
| LOW | Collect feedback from applications whether or not they convert — this is market research |

**The tone of the invitation:**
- Not: "Limited spots available! Don't miss out!"
- Yes: "I've built something for a specific kind of person. If this sounds like you, here's how to apply."

---

### Week 11–12: Close and Learn

| Priority | Task |
|----------|------|
| HIGH | Close your first cohort / engagement with the clients who are the right fit |
| HIGH | Document what made the right clients apply (specific content? specific language? specific moment in the sequence?) |
| HIGH | Send a "thank you and here's what's next" newsletter to your depth layer |
| MEDIUM | Plan Month 4+ content based on what you learned from applications |
| MEDIUM | Begin designing your entry-level digital product (can be built from landmark content you've already created) |
| LOW | Set your cadence for how often you'll run the primary offer going forward |

**Month 3 milestone check:**
- [ ] First offer opened with invitation (not a launch)
- [ ] At least 5–10 applications received
- [ ] First clients accepted and onboarded
- [ ] I understand what content drove the applications
- [ ] Entry-level offer in production

## Ongoing Cadence (Month 4+)

After the 90-day foundation, your operating rhythm looks like this:

```
WEEKLY
├── 1 major discovery content piece (landmark / vision / craft)
├── 1 newsletter issue (depth layer)
└── Respond to DMs / application replies (async)

MONTHLY
├── 1 newsletter that deepens the identity of your audience
├── Review content resonance (what's getting real responses?)
└── Open offer slots if running ongoing cohorts

QUARTERLY
├── New landmark concept development
├── Offer refinement based on client results
├── Review brand architecture — does it still fit where you're going?
└── Optional: publish an entry-level product or resource

ANNUALLY
├── Full identity review — has your goal evolved?
├── Audit your landmark map — what new territory needs to be claimed?
└── Price review — are you pricing for quality over quantity?
```

## The Only Rule That Matters

**Build from identity, not from pressure.**

Every time you feel the pull to:
- Post something just to stay visible
- Add a tactic because someone else is doing it
- Discount or change your offer because it's not filling fast enough
- Chase a trend because your content got less traction

— come back to your identity document from Phase 1.

Ask: "Is this consistent with the world I'm building, or am I reacting to the noise?"

The specialist chases the algorithm. The world builder builds the world. These are irreconcilable paths. Choose one and stay on it.

## Final Note

This framework is not a system you complete and move on from. The visible layer is always evolving — new landmarks, new offers, new platforms. But the invisible layer compounds. Every month you spend clarifying your identity, your principles, your lived path — that compounds into a depth that becomes genuinely impossible to commoditize or replicate.

You are not building a content channel. You are building a world.

The people who belong in it will find their way there. Your job is to keep building it with integrity.
